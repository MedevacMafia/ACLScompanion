Cardiac Arrest Algorithm

VF / pVT / PEA / Asystole Interactive Care Tree

1. Initiate CPR & Basic Life Support

Give oxygen. Attach monitor / defibrillator immediately. Assess rhythm type.

Shockable Rhythm (VF / pVT)

Shock Recommended

  • Deliver Shock 1: Biphasic: Device-specific (120-200 J) or maximum. Monophasic: 360 J
  • Resume CPR Immediately: Do not check pulse. Continue for 2 minutes. Establish IV/IO access.

    • Rhythm Check - Still Shockable?

  • Deliver Shock 2: Resume CPR 2 min immediately.
  • Administer Epinephrine: 1 mg IV/IO. Repeat every 3-5 minutes.
  • Consider advanced airway if needed.

    • Rhythm Check - Still Shockable?

  • Deliver Shock 3: Resume CPR 2 min immediately.
  • Administer Amiodarone: First dose 300 mg bolus IV/IO. OR Lidocaine: First dose 1 to 1.5 mg/kg IV/IO.
  • Investigate and treat reversible causes (H's and T's).

    • Subsequent Loops

  • Amiodarone (Second Dose): 150 mg IV/IO once. OR Lidocaine (Second Dose): 0.5 to 0.75 mg/kg IV/IO.
  • Epinephrine 1 mg alternating every 3-5 min.

    • Non-Shockable Rhythm (Asystole / PEA)

    No Shock Recommended

  • Administer Epinephrine IMMEDIATELY: 1 mg IV/IO. Repeat every 3-5 minutes.
  • Resume CPR Immediately: Continue for 2 minutes.
  • Establish secure IV/IO access. Consider advanced airway.

    • Rhythm Check - Still Non-Shockable?

  • Resume CPR: Continue for 2 minutes.
  • Check for pulse, verify flatline in a second lead.
  • Identify Reversible Causes: Search & treat aggressively.

    • Rhythm Check - Shockable Now?

    If rhythm converts to shockable, cross over to the Left Path (VF/pVT Shock 1). Otherwise, continue PEA/Asystole management cycle.

    Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC)

    Defined by return of pulse and BP, sudden sustained increase in PETCO2 (typically ≥ 40 mm Hg), or spontaneous arterial pressure waves.

  • Optimize ventilation and oxygenation (Keep SpO2 92%-98%; PaCO2 35-45 mm Hg).
  • Treat hypotension (SBP < 90 mm Hg): IV/IO Bolus, Epinephrine or Norepinephrine infusion, or Dopamine infusion.
  • Obtain 12-Lead ECG: STEMI? Perform emergent coronary reperfusion.
  • If patient does not follow commands: Initiate Targeted Temperature Management (TTM) (32°C - 36°C for 24 hours).

    • Reversible Causes: The H's and T's Checklist

    5 H's

    • Hypovolemia Check for flat neck veins, treat with rapid IV fluids.
    • Hypoxia Ensure patent airway, confirm oxygenation/ventilation.
    • Hydrogen Ion (Acidosis) Check arterial blood gas; consider Sodium Bicarbonate.
    • Hypo- / Hyperkalemia EKG signs (T-waves). Give Calcium Chloride/gluconate, insulin + D50.
    • Hypothermia Measure core temperature; treat with active core rewarming.

    5 T's

    • Tension Pneumothorax Tracheal deviation, asymmetric breath sounds. Perform needle decompression.
    • Tamponade (Cardiac) Muffled heart sounds, distended neck veins. Perform pericardiocentesis.
    • Toxins (Drug Overdose) TCAs, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, opioids (Narcan).
    • Thrombosis (Pulmonary) PE signs. Consider surgical embolectomy or fibrinolytics.
    • Thrombosis (Coronary) ACS/STEMI. Reperfusion (Angioplasty/PCI) is key.

    Bradycardia Algorithm

    Symptomatic Heart Rate < 50 BPM Treatment Flow

    1. Initial Clinical Assessment

    Heart rate typically < 50/min. Assess appropriateness for clinical condition.

  • Maintain airway; assist breathing as necessary.
  • Give oxygen if hypoxemic (SpO2 < 94%).
  • Monitor ECG, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation.
  • Establish IV access and obtain a 12-lead ECG.

    • 2. Evaluate for Signs of Instability

    Are any of the following present due to the bradycardia?

    • Hypotension (Systolic Blood Pressure < 90 mm Hg)
    • Acutely altered mental status
    • Signs of shock
    • Ischemic chest discomfort
    • Acute heart failure

    Patient is STABLE

    No checkboxes marked above.

  • Action: Monitor and observe.
  • Obtain comprehensive history and cardiologist consult if needed.
  • Check for drug causes (Beta-blockers, CCBs, Digoxin).

    • Patient is UNSTABLE

    Immediate intervention required (Checkboxes are marked above).

  • Atropine (First Line): 1 mg IV bolus. Repeat every 3-5 minutes (Maximum cumulative dose: 3 mg).
  • If Atropine is ineffective:
    • Transcutaneous Pacing (TCP) immediately, OR
    • Dopamine Infusion: 5 to 20 mcg/kg/min. Taper slowly, OR
    • Epinephrine Infusion: 2 to 10 mcg/min.
  • Expert consultation; prepare for transvenous pacing.

    • Tachycardia Algorithm

    Symptomatic Heart Rate ≥ 150 BPM Treatment Flow

    1. Initial Stabilization

    Heart rate typically ≥ 150/min. Assess appropriateness for clinical condition.

  • Maintain airway; assist breathing as necessary.
  • Monitor oxygen saturation (target SpO2 ≥ 94%).
  • Monitor ECG, blood pressure, and establish IV access.

    • 2. Evaluate for Signs of Instability

    Are any of the following present due to the rapid heart rate?

    • Hypotension (Systolic Blood Pressure < 90 mm Hg)
    • Acutely altered mental status
    • Signs of shock
    • Ischemic chest discomfort
    • Acute heart failure

    UNSTABLE Pathway

    Synchronized Cardioversion

  • Perform immediate Synchronized Cardioversion.
    • Narrow Regular: 50-100 J
    • Narrow Irregular: 120-200 J biphasic
    • Wide Regular: 100 J
    • Wide Irregular: Defibrillation dose (not synchronized)
  • Consider sedation prior to cardioversion.
  • If narrow regular, consider Adenosine: 6 mg rapid IV push (follow with saline flush). If no conversion, give 12 mg.

    • STABLE Pathway (Determine QRS Width)

    Is the QRS complex wide (≥ 0.12 seconds) or narrow?

  • First Line: Vagal maneuvers (Valsalva, carotid sinus massage).
  • Adenosine (if regular): 6 mg rapid IV push. If fails to convert, give 12 mg rapid IV push.
  • Beta-Blocker or Calcium Channel Blocker:
    • Diltiazem (Cardizem): 15-20 mg (0.25 mg/kg) IV over 2 min, then infusion 5-15 mg/hr.
    • Metoprolol: 5 mg slow IV at 5-min intervals (max 15 mg).
  • Only if Regular and Monomorphic: Consider Adenosine 6 mg, then 12 mg.
  • Antiarrhythmic Infusions (Stable Wide-Complex Tachycardia):
    • Amiodarone: 150 mg IV over 10 minutes. Repeat as needed if VT recurs. Follow by maintenance infusion of 1 mg/min for first 6 hours.
    • Procainamide: 20 to 50 mg/min IV until arrhythmia suppressed, hypotension ensues, QRS duration increases by 50%, or max dose (17 mg/kg) reached.
    • Sotalol: 100 mg (1.5 mg/kg) over 5 minutes. Avoid if prolonged QT.

    • Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

    Suspected Ischemia/Infarction Assessment & Reperfusion Strategy

    1. Pre-Hospital / Immediate ED Actions

  • Oxygen: Titrate to ≥ 90% (only if hypoxemic, dyspneic, or in heart failure).
  • Aspirin: 162 mg to 325 mg non-enteric coated (chewed).
  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4 mg sublingual/spray every 5 minutes (max 3 doses). Do not use if SBP < 90, or phosphodiesterase inhibitors (e.g., sildenafil) used within 24-48 hours.
  • Morphine: IV if chest pain is unresponsive to nitroglycerin (use with caution).

    • 2. Immediate ED Assessment (≤ 10 Minutes)

  • Check vital signs, establish IV access.
  • Perform and interpret 12-lead ECG immediately.
  • Obtain initial cardiac biomarkers (Troponins).
  • Perform portable chest X-ray (≤ 30 min).

    • 3. ECG Stratification and Immediate Pathway

    Classify the patient based on ECG findings to view the treatment protocol:

  • Infarction confirmed: ST elevation ≥ 1 mm in ≥ 2 contiguous leads, or new LBBB.
  • Reperfusion Goals: Time is muscle!
    • PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention): First Medical Contact (FMC)-to-device goal ≤ 90 minutes (or ≤ 120 minutes if transferred).
    • Fibrinolysis: Door-to-needle goal ≤ 30 minutes (if PCI is not available within 120 minutes). Check Fibrinolytic Checklist for contraindications.
  • Initiate adjunctive therapies as indicated: Heparin, Clopidogrel (Plavix), Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitors.
  • Ischemia suggested: ST-segment depression or dynamic T-wave inversion. High risk if persistent chest pain or elevated cardiac biomarkers (Troponin).
  • Admit & Assess: Check risk stratification score (e.g., TIMI, GRACE).
  • Therapies: Anticoagulant therapy (Unfractionated/LMWH), antiplatelets (Aspirin + Clopidogrel/Ticagrelor), Beta-blockers, Nitrates.
  • Consider early invasive strategy within 24-72 hours if high-risk features are present.
  • Normal or non-diagnostic ECG changes: Serial ECGs and serial Troponins are required.
  • Observe in an ED chest pain unit or diagnostic holding unit.
  • Perform non-invasive stress testing or coronary CT angiography if biomarker and ECG trends remain negative.

    • Acute Ischemic Stroke

    Suspected Stroke Assessment & Door-to-Needle Timeline

    1. Out-of-Hospital Assessment (FAST)

  • F - Facial Droop: Have patient show teeth or smile.
  • A - Arm Drift: Have patient close eyes and hold arms straight out.
  • S - Speech Abnormality: Have patient say "you can't teach an old dog new tricks".
  • T - Time: Note time of onset (Last Known Normal). Establish immediate transport.

    • 2. Immediate Emergency Dept. Actions (≤ 25 Min)

  • Assess ABCs and administer oxygen if hypoxic (SpO2 < 94%).
  • Perform neurologic screening (e.g., **NIH Stroke Scale**).
  • **Order Emergent Non-contrast Head CT or MRI** (to rule out hemorrhage).
  • Obtain blood glucose level immediately (Treat hypoglycemia!).

    • 3. Interpret Head CT Scan results

    Determine if the stroke is Ischemic or Hemorrhagic:

  • Check time of symptom onset (Last Known Normal).
  • **Candidate for Fibrinolysis (Alteplase/tPA)?**
    • Must be within 3 hours (or up to 4.5 hours in selected patients) of symptom onset.
    • Check Blood Pressure: Goal SBP ≤ 185 mm Hg and DBP ≤ 110 mm Hg before giving fibrinolytic.
    • Go to **Fibrinolytic Checklist** to rule out contraindications.
  • **Alteplase (tPA) Dosing:** 0.9 mg/kg (maximum 90 mg). Infuse 10% of total dose as an initial bolus over 1 minute, and the remaining 90% over 60 minutes.
  • **Tenecteplase (TNK) Option:** 0.25 mg/kg (maximum 25 mg) single IV bolus.
  • If not candidate for fibrinolytic: Administer aspirin (if no bleeding or contraindications) and manage risk factors.
  • **DO NOT adminster fibrinolytics, aspirin, or heparin.**
  • Consult neurosurgeon/neurologist immediately.
  • Control blood pressure aggressively to prevent hematoma expansion.
  • Reverse any existing anticoagulation (e.g., Protamine for Heparin, Idarucizumab for Dabigatran, Andexanet Alfa for Factor Xa inhibitors, Vit K/PCC for Warfarin).
  • Admit to Neuro-ICU or stroke unit.

    • Stroke Care Timeline Targets

  • Door to Physician Assessment: ≤ 10 minutes
  • Door to Stroke Team Contact: ≤ 15 minutes
  • Door to Head CT / MRI Scan: ≤ 20 minutes
  • Door to CT Scan Interpretation: ≤ 45 minutes
  • Door to Fibrinolytic Initiation (tPA/TNK): ≤ 60 minutes
  • Admission to monitored Stroke Unit bed: ≤ 3 hours

    • Fibrinolytic Therapy Checklists

    Contraindications Screen for Acute Ischemic Stroke & STEMI

    Ischemic Stroke Contraindications (Alteplase / TNK)

    If ANY of these exclusions are checked, Fibrinolytics should NOT be given.

    • Active internal bleeding
    • Incarcerated hemorrhage risk / intracranial neoplasm / AV malformation / aneurysm
    • Ischemic stroke, severe head trauma, or intracranial/intraspinal surgery within 3 months
    • Previous intracranial hemorrhage (ICH) at any point
    • Uncontrolled hypertension Systolic > 185 mm Hg or diastolic > 110 mm Hg unresponsive to aggressive antihypertensive agents.
    • Bleeding diathesis / Coagulopathy Platelets < 100,000; current anticoagulant use with INR > 1.7 or PT > 15s; or therapeutic dose of heparin within 48h with elevated aPTT.
    • Current use of direct thrombin inhibitors or factor Xa inhibitors with elevated sensitive lab tests
    • CT scan showing clear multilobar infarction (hypodensity > 1/3 of cerebral hemisphere)

    STEMI Fibrinolytic Contraindications

    Absolute Contraindications (DO NOT administer fibrinolytics)

    • Any prior history of intracranial hemorrhage (ICH)
    • Known structural cerebral vascular lesion (e.g., AV malformation)
    • Known malignant intracranial neoplasm (primary or metastatic)
    • Ischemic stroke within 3 months (EXCEPT acute ischemic stroke within 4.5 hours)
    • Suspected aortic dissection
    • Active bleeding or bleeding diathesis (excluding menses)
    • Significant closed-head or facial trauma within 3 months
    • Intracranial or intraspinal surgery within 2 months
    • Severe uncontrolled hypertension unresponsive to emergency therapy (SBP > 180 or DBP > 110)

    Relative Contraindications (Weigh Risk vs Benefit)

    • History of chronic, severe, poorly controlled hypertension
    • Significant hypertension on presentation (SBP > 180 or DBP > 110)
    • History of prior ischemic stroke > 3 months ago
    • Traumatic or prolonged CPR (> 10 minutes) or major surgery (< 3 weeks)
    • Recent internal bleeding (within 2-4 weeks)
    • Non-compressible vascular punctures
    • Pregnancy
    • Active peptic ulcer disease
    • Oral anticoagulant therapy (higher INR increases risk)
